8 Successful Female Musicians Who Made It Big In Hollywood

Posted by Reinaldo Massengill on Friday, September 27, 2024

Oftentimes, fans find it hard to believe all the talent and hard work that female musicians have to endure in order to succeed in the music business. Not only are they subject to discrimination, targeted harassment, and unequal pay, but they also have to navigate the trials and tribulations of being overly sexualized and being brushed over by labels. It always seems like female musicians need to work ten times as hard to establish themselves, so it is only more admirable when these musicians continue to break more glass ceilings by succeeding in Hollywood as well!

Usually, talented people who are fired by passion have endless ambitions, and these performers did not hesitate to pursue their passion for acting and filmmaking. Movies take fans on journeys just as music does, so it feels natural to see these female musicians seamlessly transition into the world of acting. Some of them made it big earning accolades and critical acclaim, others are still figuring their way to conquering both the world of music and the world of film and television.

8 Awkwafina Had Her Acting Debut On Crazy Rich Asians

Born in New York, Nora Lum A.K.A Awkwafina gained initial fame for being a rapper and comedian. Most fans are familiar with her satirical and fun songs off her two albums Yellow Ranger and In Fina We Trust. The rapper got her acting debut on the all female remake of Ocean's 8, and her debut was not free of controversy. But she then went on to have a role in the hit movie Crazy Rich Asians in addition to hosting SNL! She's slated to appear in numerous blockbuster films like The Little Mermaid remake and Shang-Chi.

7 Taylor Swift Has Dabbled In Acting From A Very Young Age

Love her or hate her, there is no denying that Taylor Swift has an immense capacity for creating compelling stories through her music. The iconic musician landed her first acting role way back in 2010 in the rom-com Valentine's Day. She also had minor roles in The Giver, The Lorax, and most recently in Amsterdam alongside Margot Robbie and Christian Bale. Taylor Swift's acting career however is bigger than many fans think, with the singer expressing that she's taking up directing seriously as she has acted in and directed her own music videos before.

6 Barbra Streisand Is A Hallmark In The World Of Music And Film

Those who aren't aware of Barbra Streisand's immense talent in acting and singing must be living under a rock. With an impressive 36 studio albums and with more than 90 million records worldwide, Streisand is one of the best-selling artists of all time. Her contribution to film is just as impressive with her starring in some beloved films like Funny Girl, A Star Is Born, and Meet The Fockers.

5 Mandy Moore Became Even More Beloved After Taking Up Acting

Mandy Moore was making music since way back in the day in 1999 when she dropped her first single Candy off her first album, So Real. However, once she starred in Tangled and This Is Us, her popularity meter skyrocketed after she had taken a hiatus from singing.

4 Jennifer Hudson Wowed Fans With Both Her Vocal Talent And Her Acting Chops

Words fail to describe the beauty and intensity of Jennifer Hudson's voice. She is a force to be reckoned with as a vocalist, and an absolute knockout as an actress. The singer wowed fans on Dream Girls and did that once more playing Aretha Franklin in Respect.

3 Beyoncé Is A Triple Threat And Will Absolutely Go Down In History

Fans know her and love her, Beyoncé has already proven that she can pretty much do anything, from dancing in stilettos to running multi-million dollar businesses. She also starred alongside Jennifer Hudson in Dream Girls, in the Lion King remake, in Austin Powers, and in practically all her innovative music videos.

2 For Some People, They First Knew Jennifer Lopez From Her Films, Not Her Music

For some fans, their first introduction to Jennifer Lopez was seeing her as an actress, not a musician. She began her career in acting at the ripe old age of 16, and after landing a few movie roles, she became a dancer and a backup vocalist for Janet Jackson. Her acting career took off simultaneously with the release of her first album in 1999, and the world has since been JLO's stage.

1 Lady Gaga Silenced All Haters And Truly Shined In A Star Is Born

Lady Gaga has always had an endless thirst for performing, whether it be music or acting. She was very open about her being bullied at school with her classmates trashing her ambitions to become an actress. Needless to say, she proved her bullies wrong. Gaga was already a hallmark in the world of pop music with albums like Born This Way topping charts worldwide. But her breakout role in the critically acclaimed film A Star Is Born made her an absolute name to be reckoned with in Hollywood as she proved to be more than capable as an actress. She was nominated for a Best Actress Academy Award, and won The Best Original Song Oscar for her song Shallow that was featured in the film.
