Understanding Net Worth

Before delving into the specifics of Ruth Bader Ginsburg’s net worth, it is important to understand what net worth means. Net worth is the value of all assets owned minus any debts or liabilities. For Ginsburg, this would include her savings, investments, property, and any income from books or other projects.

Salary as a Supreme Court Justice

One of the primary sources of Ginsburg’s wealth was her salary as a Supreme Court Justice. At the time of her passing in September 2020, the annual salary for an Associate Justice was approximately $255,300. Over her 27 years on the bench, this salary contributed significantly to her net worth.

Book Deals and Royalties

Ginsburg authored several books throughout her career, including “My Own Words,” a collection of her speeches and writings. The royalties from these publications added to her wealth. Posthumously, the interest in her works has likely increased, potentially boosting her estate’s earnings from these sources.

Investments and Savings

Like many individuals, Ginsburg would have had investments in stocks, bonds, and other financial instruments. Her financial disclosures as a Supreme Court Justice indicated that she had a diversified portfolio, which would have contributed to her net worth.

Real Estate Holdings

Real estate is often a significant component of net worth. Ginsburg owned a home in Washington, D.C., and possibly other properties. The value of these assets would be factored into her net worth.
