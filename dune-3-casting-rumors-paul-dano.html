Fans Are Already Buzzing Over The Potential Casting For Dune 3

Posted by Reinaldo Massengill on Friday, August 23, 2024

Highlights

  • Fans hope to see Paul Dano play Scytale in a potential Dune 3, as his villainous roles have received critical acclaim.
  • Scytale, central to the Dune series, can transform into others, making him a vital antagonist in the storyline.
  • Director Villeneuve plans a Dune Messiah film, confirming a third Dune movie is in the works, with some details yet to be revealed.

There appears to be a consensus among supporters of Paul Dano that he would fit a particular role in a potential future Dune film. Following the immense success of Dune I & II, it is reported that work has already begun on a third installment, although details remain scarce.

Dano has gained prominence thanks to his ability to play villainous roles with aplomb. As The Riddler in Matt Reeves’ The Batman, for instance, he went full method, and consequently delivered an acclaimed performance. It is a similarly nefarious character that fans want to see Dano portray if the Dune franchise continues.

Fans Think That Paul Dano Would Make A Perfect Scytale In A Potential Dune 3

The character that fans want to see Paul Dano play in a future Dune film is the villain Scytale. Director Denis Villeneuve has expressed interest in making a third Dune film, based on Dune Messiah, the second novel in the Frank Herbert series from which the films were adapted.

Scytale serves as a pivotal antagonist in the film, who has the power to transform into other people. This skill relies on his understanding of his marks’s psychological makeup, enabling seamless integration into his assumed identity. He is depicted in the book as a Tleilaxu Face Dancer, deeply entrenched in a conspiracy against Paul Atreides.

Later on in the plot, he assumes the guise of Lichna, daughter of Fremen Otheym, to infiltrate the Atreides Keep and orchestrate a dangerous scheme. The character's name is believed to reflect this multifaceted nature, and draws from historical and zoological references.

Scytale's narrative extends beyond the initial Dune installment, however, featuring prominently in subsequent novels by Frank Herbert and continuation works by his son Brian, as well as Kevin J. Anderson. Martin McDougall portrayed the character in the 2003 miniseries Frank Herbert's Children of Dune.

What Have Fans Said About Paul Dano As Scytale In A Possible Dune Messiah Film?

The conversation about Paul Dano playing Scytale in a potential Dune Messiah film kicked off on Reddit, after one user suggested Ryan Gosling for the role. A commenter responded, "Imagine my reaction seeing you say Ryan Gosling lol... When I really think about it, Paul Dano would be pretty cool."

Another fan chimed in, stating, "Dano can be so calculating and scary, I can see this being amazing," while a different user emphatically added, "Always Paul Dano. Every time."

Dune: Part One was released in the United States on October 22, 2021, with the sequel premiering in London on February 15, 2024. In that time period, Dano has starred in a number of films. He played a voice role as Matthew Fontenot in Jake Gyllenhaal's The Guilty in 2021.

When Could A Potential Dune Messiah Film Be Released?

In April 2024, Entertainment Weekly reported on the confirmation of a third Dune film, based on the novel Dune Messiah. According to the same feature, Denis Villeneuve first told EW in 2021 that his goal all along was to make a trilogy of Dune movies.

Everything We Know About Dune 3 So Far

  • In April 2024, it was confirmed that Legendary was already developing a third Dune movie.
  • Denis Villeneuve will reportedly take a break before continuing with the Dune Messiah adaptation, but confirms that work on the third installment is underway.
  • No new cast additions confirmed (as of March 2024).
  • (Reportedly) After Dune 3, Villeneuve and Legendary will turn their attentions to adapting Annie Jacobsen‘s nonfiction book Nuclear War: A Scenario.

"I always envisioned three movies," the director said. "It's not that I want to do a franchise, but this is Dune, and Dune is a huge story. In order to honor it, I think you would need at least three movies. That would be the dream. To follow Paul Atreides and his full arc would be nice."

Before the confirmation, and ahead of Dune: Part Two's release, legendary composer Hans Zimmer (who scored both films) also revealed that he was already writing music for a third. He also said that Villeneuve had actually completed and presented him with a copy of Dune Messiah, the movie.

That being said, as of March 2024, there had not even been communication about when production is expected to start. Consequently, fans might have to wait a few good years before they can enjoy Dune Messiah on the big screen.
