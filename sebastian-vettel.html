    Sebastian Vettel Facts

  • Vettel got into amateur karting at the age of 3½ years. By the age of 8, he was participating in the karts series.
  • As his talent became well evident, he was accepted into the Red Bull Junior Team at the age of 11 years. He managed to win numerous titles including Junior Monaco Kart Cup in 2001 with the Red Bull team.
  • As his performances became too big for the junior team, he was promoted to open-wheel cars in 2003.
  • In 2004, he won the German Formula BMW Championship after having won 18 out of 20 races. Because of this, he was given an opportunity to test a Reynard Motorsport Champ Car by Derrick Walker, a former British auto racing team owner.
  • In 2006, the 18-year-old Vettel was promoted as a test driver for Sauber in Formula One.
  • Before the 2006 Turkish Grand Prix, BMW Sauber appointed him as the third driver. On his testing debut at the event, he became the youngest Formula One driver to participate in a Grand Prix weekend.
  • At the 2007 Japanese Grand Prix, he crashed into Mark Webber‘s car, taking both out of the race. At that time, Webber was leading the race and Vettel was in the 3rd position behind Lewis Hamilton. After the race, he was handed a 10-place grid penalty for the following race. However, the penalty was lifted after the YouTube video revealed that Hamilton’s behavior behind the safety car was the real cause of the accident.
  • At the end of the 2008 season, his great performances were recognized as the “Rookie of the Year” at the Autosport Awards.
  • At the end of the 2012 season, he won his 3rd consecutive world championship. He also became the first racer to have won the triple championship at the age of 25.
  • In 2010, he was named “German Sports Personality of the Year” (Sportler des Jahres) through a voting process.
  • In 2009, he was voted as the “F1 Driver of the Year”. He again won the title in 2011 and 2013. He had finished as a runner-up in 2010 and 2012.
  • In 2013, he was recognized as the “BBC Overseas Sports Personality of the Year”.
  • Early in his life, he wanted to be a singer just like Michael Jackson. But, had to drop the idea when he realized he didn’t have a singer’s voice.
  • For the 2021 and 2022 seasons, he left Ferrari to race for Aston Martin.
  • In February 2022, Vettel publicly announced that he would not race in the Russian Grand Prix, which was scheduled to happen in September 2022, after Russia had launched the military operation in Ukraine.
  • In April 2022, he was fined 5,000 euros for riding a scooter on the Albert Park circuit after the conclusion of Free Practice 1 for the Australian Grand Prix. He had broken Article 26.7 of the Sporting Regulations of Formula One.
  • In July 2022, Sebastian Vettel announced his retirement from Formula One racing at the end of the 2022 season. Fernando Alonso had replaced Vettel at the start of the 2023 season.
