Steve Stricker Illness: What Health Condition Does He Have?

Posted by Larita Shotwell on Sunday, August 25, 2024

This article gives an update on the health condition of Steve Stricker, an American golfer well known for his accomplishments.

A well-known American golfer known for his accomplishments on the PGA Tour and PGA Tour Champions is Steven Stricker.

He achieved a career-high world ranking of No. 2 in September 2009. He has spent more than 250 weeks in the top 10 of the Official World Golf Ranking.

What is the illness of Steve Stricker?

Late in 2022, Stricker fell unwell with an unidentified ailment. He experienced flu-like symptoms, jaundice, and an erratic pulse.

He first developed a persistent fever, a sore throat, and other unsettling symptoms.

He tested negative for Covid-19, but his illness worsened, so he went to UW Health University Hospital for treatment.

Stricker struggled with issues like a high white blood cell count, a low liver count, jaundice, and swelling in his lips and tongue while he was in the hospital.

He also had an abnormal pulse and myocarditis or pericarditis, which is inflammation around the heart.

Stricker lost 25 pounds and lost the ability to walk more than a few steps as his health deteriorated.

Stricker made a spectacular return to the PGA Tour Champions after spending almost two weeks in the hospital, finishing second at the Insperity Invitational in April.

He was well enough by Christmas to celebrate with his family. He hasn’t disclosed any significant health relapses since then.
