Bob Baffert

In 1970, Bob Baffert started his career as a racer on legalized tracks at the age of 17. After his marriage, he started as a trainer at Quarter Horses a farm in Prescott, Arizona.

After that, he had established himself as a trainer by the age of twenty. In the 1980s, he shifted to southern California and started training Thoroughbred horses at the Los Alamitos Race Course.

Additionally, he purchased Thirty Slews, his first Thoroughbred horse, in 1988. Moreover, in 1992, Thirty Slews won his first Breeder’s Cup race.

Furthermore, he became the top money-winning trainer in the US four times. In 2018, he won his second Triple Crown. Authentic, the Kentucky Derby winner in 2020, was also trained by him.

He worked with several high-profile clients, including John C. Mabee’s Golden Eagle Farm, Prince Ahmed bin Salman’s Thoroughbred Corporation, Robert and Janice McNair

, Mike Pegram, and the late Bob Lewis and his wife, Beverly.  In 2014, Bob won his first Breeder’s Cup Classic, racing with Bayern, with the help of horse owner Kaleem Shah.

He has previously worked as a trainer for Zayat Stables and Juddmonte Farms.
