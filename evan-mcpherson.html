Evan McPherson- Biography

Evan McPherson is a kicker with the Cincinnati Bengals of the National Football League (NFL) in the United States (NFL). Before being selected by the Bengals in the fifth round of the NFL Draft as the overall 149th pick in 2021, he attended the University of Florida. In the first round of the draft, he was the only kicker selected.

Evan McPherson- Birth, Age, Ethnicity, Siblings

Evan McPherson is a 23-year-old actor who was born on July 21, 1999. Logan, his older brother, was an all-state kicker at Fort Payne High School and eventually played punter for Louisiana Tech University. In 2019, Evan’s younger brother, Alex, was named to the All-State kicker and punter team for Fort Payne. Alex has expressed his desire to play football for Auburn University in 2021. Alex is currently the top kicker in the class of 2022.

Evan McPherson started his football career at Fort Payne High School in Fort Payne, Alabama. He was the kicker and punter for the team during his four years with them. At Fort Payne, he kicked a 60-yard field goal and punted an 84-yarder. In Alabama, both kicks set a new high school record. He was chosen to the first-team All-State team in 2017. McPherson attended Florida from 2018 to 2020 before opting out of his senior year to enter the NFL draft. At Florida, McPherson had an overall record of 51-60 field goals, which was one of the best career field goal percentages in the SEC. In his career, he kicked 149-150 extra points and 5 of 8 50-yard field goals.

Evan McPherson- Professional Career

McPherson was chosen 149th overall in the fifth round of the NFL draft in 2021 by the Cincinnati Bengals. He went 5 for 5 on field goals in the Bengals’ three preseason games in 2021. He also kicked a field goal from 57 yards out. After firing Austin Seibert, the Bengals’ only kicker, at the end of the preseason, McPherson was appointed the starting kicker for the regular season in 2021. McPherson was the first player from Fort Payne High School to appear in a regular-season NFL game. McPherson finished the season with a 28/33 field goal %, an 84.8 average percentage, and 9 out of 11 field goals inside the 50-yard line.

In a playoff game against the Las Vegas Raiders, he kicked four field goals, guiding his team to a 26-19 victory. The victory was called the first in the playoffs in 31 years. In the divisional round, McPherson was 4/4 on field goals, including two 50-yarders. He kicked a 52-yard field goal as time expired. As a consequence of this play, the Bengals defeated the Titans 19-16. For the first time since 1988, he led his team to the AFC Championship Game in January 2022.
