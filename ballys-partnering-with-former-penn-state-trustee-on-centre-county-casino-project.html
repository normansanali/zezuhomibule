Bally's partnering with former Penn State trustee on Centre County casino project

Posted by Martina Birk on Friday, August 9, 2024

CENTRE COUNTY, Pa. (WJAC) â€” A new casino project in Centre County is moving along.

Bally's Corporation announced they're a partner for a new casino near the Nittany Mall.

Bally's is working with former Penn State trustee, Ira Lubert.

He was awarded a mini-casino license last year by the state's gaming control board.

Lubert was previously involved in a proposal to locate a casino in the mall's former Bon-Ton store site.

Now there's word they're eyeing the former Macy's store location.

Bally's says construction on the $120 million project will begin later this year.

The casino plans include up to 350 slot machines and 30 gaming tables.
